How to use this tool / glossary

Quick start

  • Refresh BC data: click "🔄 Refresh from BC" at the top. This pulls the latest sales revenue and Vendor Ledger (AP) data from BigQuery via the connected Google Sheet. Runs nightly anyway, but the button forces it now.
  • Add UPS invoices: Sign into UPS Billing Centre → My Invoices → set Status filter to "All" (otherwise newer Open / Past Due invoices stay hidden). Click each invoice's three-dot Actions menu → "Download CSV (250 Columns)". Drop the CSVs onto this page.
  • Add DHL invoices: Sign into DHL MyBill → Archive → download the CSV for each invoice. Drop the CSVs onto this page.
  • Rate cards (optional): click 📊 Rate card to upload the UPS rate card (.xls) and/or DHL rate card (.xlsx). Once uploaded, the Shipments tab shows Expected and Variance columns so you can see whether the carrier billed correctly.
  • The tool runs in your browser. Carrier invoice files never leave your computer. BC data is stored on Cloudflare KV (shared across team viewers).

What each tab shows

Dashboard
KPI tiles (Invoices, Invoices Due, Invoices Overdue, Total Net, Shipping Revenue, Margin, etc.) plus anomalies and top routes. Click an Invoices Due or Invoices Overdue tile to drill straight to the detail tab.
By Category
Total net charges grouped by the categories defined below (Freight, Fuel, Service Surcharges, etc.). Shows where your money goes.
By Carrier Invoice
One row per carrier invoice with sub-totals per charge category and a Status column (Paid / Due / Overdue / —) matched against BC's Vendor Ledger. Click a row to expand and see every charge line on that invoice.
Due Invoices
Open UPS / DHL invoices not yet past their due date. Sorted closest-to-becoming-overdue first. Useful for AP planning.
Overdue Invoices
Open UPS / DHL invoices past their due date. Sorted worst-first. CSV download is emailable straight to AP.
Shipments
One row per tracking number, with Expected freight and Variance against the uploaded rate card. Click any row to see the full charge breakdown.
Top Surcharges
The biggest non-freight charges across the period. Helpful for identifying recurring fees worth negotiating.
Monthly Trend
Charge totals month by month. Lets you spot seasonal effects or sudden cost jumps.
P&L by SO
For each sales order: revenue from BC vs total carrier cost = margin. "Matched" means BC posted revenue AND we have the carrier invoice. "Pending" means carrier invoice exists but BC hasn't posted revenue yet. "Unbilled" means we shipped something but never invoiced the customer.
Monthly P&L
Same as P&L by SO but rolled up by month, in two views: Cash-flow (when money moved) and Matching (when the shipment happened).
Unbilled Shipments
Shipments where the SO ref looks like a real customer order but BC has no matching sales invoice. Each row is a possible missed billing. Worth a look weekly.
Internal Shipping
Shipments with no SO ref (example-co, STOCK, blank). Treated as overhead, not customer cost. Each charge can have a tracking number / SO ref assigned manually if it should actually be billed.

Status & Variance columns

Status (By Carrier Invoice tab)
Paid — BC has closed this vendor invoice. Due — BC has it open, not yet past due date. Overdue — BC has it open, past due date. — — couldn't match to a BC entry (BC may not have posted it yet, or amounts don't match within tolerance).
Expected (Shipments tab)
What the rate card says the freight charge should have been for that service, destination and weight. Blank if no rate card uploaded.
Variance (Shipments tab)
Actual freight minus Expected. Positive (red) = carrier charged more than the rate card; negative (green) = less. Flagged on the Dashboard anomalies panel when £5+ OR 20%+ off — either alone is enough.

Charge categories

Freight (FRT / Weight Charge)
Base shipping charge. Driven by service level (WW Expedited, Dom. Standard, EXPRESS WORLDWIDE), zone, weight, dimensions. Net = what you pay after corporate discount; Published = list price.
Fuel Surcharge (FSC / FF)
Variable percentage on top of freight to cover fuel costs. Reset weekly. Typically 15–35% of freight. UPS uses code FSC; DHL uses FF.
Service Surcharges
Per-package shipping add-ons. UPS: Surge Fee (PFC/PFR), Additional Handling (AHC/SAH), Remote/Extended Area (HIS/ESD/ESP/LDS), Residential (RES/REP), Pickup options (OSW/OFW/ASW/AFW), Returns Pickups (ART/RSO). DHL: GoGreen Plus carbon offset (FD), Premium 12:00 / 10:30 / 9:00 (YK). Real shipping costs driven by parcel characteristics or service choices.
Documentation
Per-invoice paperwork: Paper Commercial Invoice (CIS — going paperless eliminates this), Print Label (ALP), International Processing (FIP), Duty & Tax Forwarding (F/D).
Service Issues
Penalties or unusual high-value lines: Prohibited Item Fee (PIF), Customer Solution Service Fee (CGS). Good candidates for an internal review and potential carrier dispute.
Brokerage (BRK)
Customs clearance: disbursement fees, additional handling, document posting. International shipments only.
VAT (Value Added Tax)
UPS: GOV/205 (broker-collected on imported goods value, often reclaimable) + TAX/01 (20% UK VAT on UPS service fees, reclaimable). DHL: Total Tax / TAX/A (UK VAT on DHL service fees, reclaimable).
Duty / Tax
Customs duty by HS code and country. Unlike VAT, NOT reclaimable.
Adjustment
Post-invoice corrections: rebills, refunds, audit credits, DHL discounts.
Import VAT
VAT charged on imported goods value, passed through to the customer. EXCLUDED from our cost figures because it isn't ours to absorb.

Terms worth knowing

BC (Business Central)
Microsoft's accounting system. The dashboard reads two BigQuery views via a connected Google Sheet: Posted Sales Invoice Lines (revenue) and Vendor Ledger Entries (AP / Amount Due & Overdue).
VLE (Vendor Ledger Entry)
An entry in BC's accounts payable. One per vendor invoice. Used to compute Amount Due / Overdue KPIs and to assign the Paid / Due / Overdue status on the By Carrier Invoice tab.
SO (Sales Order)
A customer order in BC. Matched to carrier shipments via the SO ref typed into the UPS / DHL shipping label.
Unique Shipments vs Shipment Lines
"Unique Shipments" = distinct tracking numbers (physical parcels). "Shipment Lines" = each tracking-invoice pair (a parcel billed across two invoices counts twice).
Matched / Pending / Unbilled SO
Matched: BC has the sales invoice AND we have the carrier invoice. Pending: carrier billed us but BC hasn't posted revenue yet (will usually clear within weeks). Unbilled: carrier billed us but BC has no matching sales invoice — we shipped but didn't invoice.
Rate card variance threshold
A freight charge is flagged when actual differs from rate-card expected by £5+ OR 20%+. Either alone is enough — catches both rare expensive overcharges and consistent small ones that add up over hundreds of shipments.
